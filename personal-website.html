A Chance to Learn

I try to approach all my projects with a clear idea of what I want to learn from working on them. During my project redesigning a local ice cream shop’s website, I learned about responding to client requirements and requests, identifying user stories, and conducting usability testing. Creating my own website was a chance to dive deeper into web development and explore core concepts of modern web design:

  1. Create a responsive design that will look as good on a phone as on a large desktop display.

  2. Carefully craft both a light and dark appearance to adapt to the user’s environment.

  3. Explore effortless interactions using animations and dynamic components.

Responsive Design

As I was looking for inspiration, I found Olivier Ifrah’s website. I loved the simplicity and playfulness of it. His card-based designed became the starting point for what I wanted to create.

When bringing the card layout to smaller screens, I found it critical to lay out the cards in a way that felt native to that device, respecting how information naturally flows on each. It was also imperative to select specific elements on each card to emphasize rather than simply shrinking all their content.

Light and Dark Appearance

Next, I was looking to design a light and dark appearance that felt individually crafted yet undoubtedly related. The cards, for example, are uniquely crafted for each appearance. In light appearance, they pop with a clear drop shadow and a lighter shade than the background. In dark appearance, the same principles are applied, but augmented with a thin light rim going around the card, making them pop a little extra.

A unique hover effect was also crafted for each appearance. I was inspired by an effect that subtly brightens the area around your cursor and lightens the thin rim around the card as the cursor moves by. This effect makes the card feel alive, responding to your cursor movement. I brought the same effect to light appearance, while adapting it to feel native to this lighter interface. The same brightening around the cursor gives the card a glossy feel, and by distinctly raising the card above the background, it feels alive.

Card hover effect in dark and light appearance

Effortless Interactions

A goal from the very beginning was to make every interaction feel as light as possible – from the navigation, to links, to menus. On mobile for example, instead of the rather heavy traditional hamburger menu, I opted for a pill-shaped card that indicates the current section of the page and expands to let the user navigate to any section.

Mobile navigation

To preserve this sense of lightness and to avoid pop-up menus from feeling too persistent, they automatically collapse when the user scrolls. That way the interface responds to the user’s intention and reveals the content hidden behind.
